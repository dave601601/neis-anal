Does school lunch follow student food trends?

Yes — but only what the FORM · INSTITUTION · BUDGET filter lets through.

What lands on the tray is set by FORM · INSTITUTION · BUDGET — not taste, not popularity, not “the big city.”
Three common beliefs all fail.

Three beliefs — and why each fails

Finding 1 · FORM

Popular ≠ on the tray. Can it become a dish?

×6.9mala growth, 2021→2025
  • Mala becomes a meal: mala-pork, mala-soup.
  • Tanghulu & Dubai chocolate barely appear — snacks can’t be a meal.
  • Mala ≈ 80% of the whole refined trend basket.

Belief “popular → on the tray” FAILS. What matters: can it be cooked as a dish?

Finding 2 · INSTITUTION

The menu is a two-way mirror, not student demand

2.9×trend rises faster than health (×5.4 vs ×1.7)
  • Both stall after 2023.
  • Health / vegan is not student demand — adults (education offices) push it top-down.
  • Students push back via food waste.

Belief “students drive the menu” FAILS — in both directions.

Finding 3 · SPACE  [GIS core]

Not the metropolis — an eating-out geography

I = +0.26Moran’s I, mala · sigungu · p = 0.001
  • Uptake is not led by Seoul/metro — in 90% of bootstraps, metro < non-metro.
  • LISA hotspots = Busan & Daegu (big cities).
  • Not the spicy-food culture space (r = −0.18): it’s an urban dining-out trend.

Belief “the big city leads trends” FAILS — and uptake is clustered, not uniform.

Why? Three mechanisms behind the filter

① Institutional filter

  • Nutrition standards forbid excess sugar/fat (별표3).
  • Monthly procurement locked by the 27th.
  • High-calorie-low-nutrition rule screens out snack-type fads.
  • Result: snacks filtered out; mala passes as a dish.

② Financial incentives (qualitative)

  • Eco-friendly extra support, e.g. Gyeongbuk 1,084 vs Seoul 0 KRW/meal.
  • May enable more menu experimentation in some provinces.
  • NOT causal: Busan = 0 KRW yet a mala hotspot — a counterexample.
  • Only 5 of 17 regions publish this data.

③ Two-way mirror

  • Vegan day is top-down (Gwangju 2011, nation’s first).
  • Students reject it: waste +30–40%, 33% dissatisfied.
  • Mandatory → optional retreat; forced-without-prep is rejected.
  • With prep, 6 Incheon schools reached 81% approval.
Conclusion. School lunch does mirror student trends — but only what the FORM · INSTITUTION filter lets through, and unevenly in space: mala clusters in big cities, distinct from local food culture.

Limitations

  • Basket is ~80% mala — closer to “mala adoption” than “trends” broadly.
  • Sido ranking is unstable; robust result is metro insensitivity, not a specific #1.
  • Health keywords are vegan-centric.
  • Budget data only 5/17 regions (qualitative, not causal).
  • Spatial clustering is driven by mala.
  • NEIS only from 2021 — no pre-trend baseline.
  • Regional trend↔health correlation NOT significant (r = −0.46, p = 0.07, Jeju-dependent).

Data & sources

NEIS school-lunch records (2,355 high schools, ~2.2M lunches, 2021–2025; Jeonbuk excluded). Keyword extraction via FastText embedding (root-based expansion). Spatial statistics: Moran’s I & LISA at sigungu scale (PySAL). Budget figures from 5 of 17 metropolitan/provincial education offices (public disclosures).
