Three beliefs — and why each fails

Finding 1 · FORM

Popular ≠ on the tray. Can it become a dish?

×6.9mala growth, 2021→2025
  • Mala becomes a meal (mala-pork, mala-soup).
  • Tanghulu & Dubai chocolate barely appear — snacks can’t be a meal.

Belief “popular → on the tray” FAILS. What matters: can it be cooked as a dish?

Finding 2 · INSTITUTION

A two-way mirror, not student demand

2.9×trend food vs health food (2025)
  • Health / vegan is not student demand — adults push it top-down.
  • Students push back via food waste.

Belief “students drive the menu” FAILS — in both directions.

Finding 3 · SPACE  [GIS core]

Not the metropolis — an eating-out geography

I = 0.25Moran’s I (mala, sigungu) · p < 0.001
  • Not led by Seoul or the metropolis.
  • Mala clustering is statistically significant (a real spatial pattern, not a pivot table).
  • Distinct from the local spicy-food culture — it’s an urban dining-out trend.

Belief “the big city leads trends” FAILS — uptake is clustered, not uniform.

Why? Three mechanisms behind the filter

① Institutional filter

  • Nutrition standards forbid excess sugar/fat.
  • Monthly procurement locked by the 27th.
  • High-calorie-low-nutrition rule screens out snack fads.
  • Result: snacks filtered out; mala passes as a dish.

② Financial incentives (qualitative)

  • Eco-food extra budget, e.g. Gyeongbuk 1,084 vs Seoul 0 KRW/meal.
  • May enable more menu experimentation in some provinces.
  • NOT causal: Busan = 0 KRW yet a mala hotspot — a counterexample.

③ Two-way mirror

  • Vegan day is top-down (Gwangju 2011, nation’s first).
  • Students reject it via food waste → many regions retreated to optional.
  • But with prep & education, approval rebounds (Incheon pilots 81%).
Conclusion. School lunch does mirror student trends — but only what the FORM · INSTITUTION filter lets through, and unevenly in space: mala clusters in big cities, distinct from local food culture.

Limitations

  • Trend basket is ~80% mala — read as “mala adoption.”
  • Budget link is qualitative (5/17 regions), not causal.
  • NEIS from 2021 — no pre-trend baseline.

Data & methods

NEIS lunch records (2,355 high schools, ~2.2M lunches, 2021–2025; Jeonbuk excluded). FastText embedding for keyword extraction. Spatial statistics: Moran’s I & LISA at sigungu scale (PySAL). Budget figures from 5 of 17 education offices (public disclosures). Full statistics in the paper.
